# 3D Scanning Service Providers -- Greater Manchester & UK

*Research compiled: 2026-03-31*
*Context: Cost benchmarking for LichtFeld-Studio pipeline vs commercial scanning services*

---

## Executive Summary

Commercial 3D scanning of a gallery space (100-200 sqm) in the Manchester area typically costs **GBP 1,500-5,000+** for a single session with point-cloud deliverables, or **GBP 3,000-15,000+** when BIM/CAD models are required. Individual artifact scanning ranges from **GBP 80-500+ per object** depending on complexity. By contrast, the LichtFeld-Studio Gaussian splatting pipeline produces a scene at an estimated cost of **GBP 0.18-0.40 per scene** (compute only), representing a cost reduction of roughly **3,750x to 83,000x** for room-scale capture and **200x to 2,800x** for individual objects.

## 5. Cost Comparison: Commercial Scanning vs LichtFeld-Studio Pipeline

### 5.1 Room-Scale Scene Capture

| Approach | Cost per Scene | Time | Output |
|----------|---------------|------|--------|
| **LichtFeld-Studio (3DGS pipeline)** | GBP 0.18-0.40 | Minutes (compute) | 3D Gaussian splat, novel view synthesis |
| **Matterport virtual tour** | GBP 380-760 | Half day on-site + processing | Virtual tour, point cloud |
| **LIDAR point cloud only** | GBP 1,500-5,000 | 1-2 days on-site + processing | Registered point cloud |
| **LIDAR + 3D model** | GBP 3,000-15,000 | 1-2 weeks total | Point cloud + CAD/BIM model |

**Cost multiplier (commercial vs pipeline):**
- vs Matterport: **950x - 4,200x** more expensive
- vs LIDAR point cloud: **3,750x - 27,800x** more expensive
- vs LIDAR + model: **7,500x - 83,000x** more expensive

### 5.2 Individual Object Capture

| Approach | Cost per Object | Output |
|----------|----------------|--------|
| **LichtFeld-Studio (3DGS pipeline)** | GBP 0.18-0.40 | 3D Gaussian splat |
| **Basic structured light scan** | GBP 80-180 | Mesh + texture |
| **Heritage-grade scan** | GBP 300-1,000 | High-res mesh + colour |

**Cost multiplier (commercial vs pipeline):**
- vs basic scan: **200x - 1,000x** more expensive
- vs heritage-grade: **750x - 5,500x** more expensive

### 5.3 Caveats

The comparison above is **not apples-to-apples**:

1. **Output fidelity**: Commercial LIDAR produces metrically accurate point clouds (sub-mm precision). 3DGS produces view-synthesis models that are photorealistic but not metrically calibrated without additional ground-truth constraints.
2. **Deliverable type**: Commercial services produce CAD/BIM-ready data, engineering drawings, and conservation-grade documentation. 3DGS produces novel-view renderings and radiance fields.
3. **Input requirements**: Commercial scanning requires on-site technician and specialist equipment. 3DGS pipeline requires only photographs/video (potentially smartphone-captured).
4. **Regulatory acceptance**: Heritage bodies (Historic England, HES) may require specific survey standards that only LIDAR/photogrammetry with control points can satisfy.
5. **Complementary use**: 3DGS is best positioned as a complement to (not replacement for) traditional scanning -- providing low-cost visual documentation, public engagement assets, and rapid prototyping of scenes before commissioning expensive surveys.
