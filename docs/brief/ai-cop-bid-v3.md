**Title of the project**

AI Workflow Solutions towards the Efficient Preservation of Immersive and Intangible Cultural Heritage in Virtual Spaces with DreamLab AI

**Project aims and objectives (100 word limit)**

The University has a growing catalogue of immersive and experiential interactive and time-based arts projects and research activities that happen in real-time through public-facing events – including Nested Cinema (Vera’s Not Alone), We Invented the Weekend, and Lightwaves.

However, there is currently no coherent, or efficient pipeline for the preservation and emulation of such site-based interactive and immersive experiences.

Working with local CCI business DreamLab AI, the project aims to test a novel and efficient pipeline through to games engines deploying a number of AI agential workflows on existing digital assets such as video recordings, still imagery and other existing digital assets.

**Describe fully the activities to be undertaken, including expected outcomes and outputs from each activity in the project (200 word limit)**

The project team is cross-disciplinary and has identified that, building on its existing but nascent virtual (Meta)Campus environment (MediaCity Campus ground floor, New Adelphi, Old Fire Station, Energy House2, GMIOT) in Unreal Engine, there is an opportunity to test a workflow for using a games engine as an advanced, accessible, low resolution preservation space.

1.  Stakeholder ideation and development workshops drawing from the teams’ academic and creative and digital industry networks to develop the roadmap and business case for the project. This will steer the foundational research and development, specified tasks within the timeline, and generate the key milestones.

2.  Direct consultation and tools development with local CCI AI specialists DreamLab AI, identifying efficiencies throughout the workflow, and including end user experience testing.

3.  The project is designed to enable cross-disciplinary CPD and KE for internal staff, including technical staff, with direct input from AI experts outside of the University with tangible results that give a new understanding of the business value of the proposition and new tools for demonstrating a proof of principle efficient workflow as evidence to support a business case for further development.

**Outline the impact you are aiming to create, e.g. the external partner challenge you want to address (300 word limit)**

Globally technology improves and the fidelity of experiences mediated though games engine “virtual worlds” continues to improve and become more attractive. Simultaneously the growth of the out-of-home, or “experience economy,” means more content is being produced at better quality, in combinations of technologies, and in multivalent ways for public consumption. This project bridges these two dynamic cultural and economic forces, in that it tests an approach to the efficient capturing and re-presenting of both historic and contemporary events that has widespread implications for redeploying cultural assets creatively and for economic benefit.

Working with a leading CCI AI developer in AI agential toolsets, with an existing CCI network, will support the development of new working methodologies for the efficient adaption of AI into the “virtualised” workflow. A technical report at the end of the project, alongside access to core coding and tools used in the development of the project, will provide an evidence base for furthering this research.

Additionally the project will investigate both good practice and establish contractual relationship with a private sector party, that negotiates and respects IP generated for both common good and civic value (Open Source), shared IP, and other licencing structures (such as Creative Commons) appropriate to the project for the mutual benefit of all stakeholders.

The project also deliberately aligns with plans currently in development with academics, EU and global cultural partners for an application to the EU Commission **Horizon Europe 2027 Culture, Creativity and Inclusive Society (Cluster 2) -** specifically **HORIZON-CL2-2027-01-HERITAGE-08: Safeguarding & transmission of intangible cultural heritage** - which would be a key opportunity to continue, sustain development, and scale, the project internationally. Current associated partnerships for the EU Commission funding include Rhizome (USA) and Onassis ONX (USA and Greece), with proposed partners include the Waag FutureLab (Holland) and V2 (Holland).

The EU Commission summerises the impact thus:

*The ICH Convention was adopted in 2003, by 148 countries and it recognizes the importance of ICH as a driving force for social cohesion, cultural diversity, and sustainable development. However, the nature of ICH, as living heritage, to continually transform and evolve, makes it particularly challenging to keep alive. Therefore, safeguarding ICH encounters very different challenges than the built heritage sector. ICH is rooted in territories and communities and is essential for identity building and maintaining social cohesion and a harmonious relation to the natural environment. It is a source for creativity and innovation, and a critical factor for new competitive scenarios.”*
